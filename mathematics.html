A typical Lesson

Maths lessons are 55minutes long and generally begin with a short starter designed to ensure students are regularly practicing keyskills. From here a short review of the weeks homework (if requested) takes place. To help develop students’ independent learning skills (vital for success at GCSE and above), students receive full solutions with homework questions, with which they are encouraged to self mark and reflect on their answers. This allows tutors to spend this part of the lesson focussing on addressing the main issues the homework brought up.

The bulk of the lesson focusses on the specific topics that require work. These are usually identified through a combination of an initial consultation, weekly homework, and regularly reviews between students and tutors. Each student has access to their own personal lesson and progress tracker where the exact details of each lesson topic can be viewed.

Each lesson concludes with a few minutes for the student and tutor to reflect on the work covered, progress made. Students are encouraged to actively reflect on their own learning and progress, and set achievable goals for revision (where appropriate).
